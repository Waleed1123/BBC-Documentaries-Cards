Planet Earth II

2006 300 min Science Documentary

"Planet Earth II" is a British nature documentary series produced by the BBC. It is the sequel to the highly acclaimed "Planet Earth" series, which originally aired in 2006. "Planet Earth II" was first broadcast in 2016, and like its predecessor, it was narrated by Sir David Attenborough. The series consists of six episodes, each focusing on a different habitat: "Islands," "Mountains," "Jungles," "Deserts," "Grasslands," and "Cities."

The Blue Planet

2001 390 min Science Documentary

The series consists of eight episodes, each highlighting different aspects of oceanography and marine biology. The episodes are titled "Ocean World," "Frozen Seas," "Open Ocean," "The Deep," "Seasonal Seas," "Coral Seas," "Tidal Seas," and "Coasts." The series is known for its groundbreaking underwater footage and comprehensive exploration of the diverse habitats and creatures found in the Earth's oceans.

Blue Planet II

2017 360 min Science Documentary

The series explores the world's oceans, showcasing the incredible diversity of marine life and ecosystems, while also addressing the environmental challenges faced by these environments. "Blue Planet II" consists of seven episodes: "One Ocean," "The Deep," "Coral Reefs," "Big Blue," "Green Seas," "Coasts," and "Our Blue Planet." Each episode delves into different aspects of marine life and ecosystems, using advanced filming techniques to capture breathtaking underwater footage.

Seven Worlds, One Planet

2019 360 min Science Documentary

"Seven Worlds, One Planet" is another notable nature documentary series produced by the BBC. It premiered in 2019 and, like other BBC nature documentaries, it is narrated by Sir David Attenborough. The series explores the seven continents of the world, highlighting the unique wildlife and ecosystems found on each. The seven episodes of the series are dedicated to specific continents: Asia, Australia, Antarctica, Europe, North America, South America, and Africa.

Our Planet

2019 400 min Science Documentary

"Our Planet" is a nature documentary series that was released on Netflix in 2019. It is narrated by Sir David Attenborough, who is renowned for his work in presenting and narrating wildlife documentaries. The series was produced in collaboration with Silverback Films and the World Wildlife Fund (WWF). The series consists of eight episodes, each focusing on a different habitat: "One Planet," "Frozen Worlds," "Jungles," "Coastal Seas," "From Deserts to Grasslands," "The High Seas," "Fresh Water," and "Forests."
